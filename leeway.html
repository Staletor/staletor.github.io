Leeway - emotions and ability

Norwegian University of Technology and Science 2011

A camera designed to trigger emotions through user experience and satisfaction.
Target group was women between 20-30 years old with an above average interest in photography.

In-depth user interview

Identify the emotional responses when using a camera and shooting photos. 8 people between 20-34 years old where interviewed.

The conlusion:
Both genders shows a desire to take good photos. A technical advanced design may discourage those without a technical interest. Equal desire between genders to portray an image as someone who is interested in photography. Good photos gives pride and enables the desire to evolve. Important to trigger the ability take good photos early to ensure continued use.

Shape associations test

Identify the emotional responses associated with holding and touching shapes. 8 women between 20-27 years old were tested. The test had 3 parts: without knowing the subject was cameras, knowing the subject was cameras and in the context of desired image.

The conlusion:
Safe to hold was an important emotion: "I don't want to drop my camera." Most wanted a camera that gives credibility as someone interested in photography, but shouldn't be too advanced to use.

Philosophy

With a good lens and interesting angles it is easier to shoot good photos.
Fulfill the desire to be perceived as someone interested in photography.
